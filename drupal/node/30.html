1. What are the objectives of the Sheep & Wool Improvement Scheme SWIS?

  • To increase indigenous wool production.
  • To increase the quality of indigenous wool.
  • To decrease the mortality rate of sheep.
  • To increase per wool production.
  • To train sheep breeders in sheep rearing through modern & scientific techniques.
  • To improve the breed of Indian sheep.
  • To provide support to the entire chain from shepherd to the end customer.
  • To provide better returns to domestic wool producers for their products.
  • To enhance the economic status of breeders.

2. Who can apply to implement this SWIS?

Registered NGOs/Trust/Cooperative Society/State Govt. Sheep/Wool Board, Federation, Cooperation/Sangh etc.

3. How to apply for SWIS?

The board has a prescribed format. The applicant must fill it up along with the project proposal; all attested required documents/information/data etc.

4. What are the Components of SWIS?

Support for survey and study, Registration of flocks, Project formulation, Training, Breed improvement, Health Care, Grading and marketing assistance, Setting up of Multi Purpose Extension Center & Ram Rearing Farm Existing, Overhead expenditure to implementing agency.

5. What is the Funding pattern?

CWDB provides 100% of the amount as a grant-in-aid installment-wise and as per the size of the project. The next installment of the amount of grant is released after satisfactory utilization of the 1st installment.

6. What is the project period?

3 years.

7. What are the criteria for the sanction of the proposal of SWIS?

Project committees examine the project with respect to all parameters of SWIS. The recommendation of the project committee is further put up to the Executive Committee of the CWDB for their decision.

8. What is the breed improvement component?

There are provisions to provide good quality stud Rams to the breeder to improve their sheep breed as per the concerned State Govt. Policy to improve sheep breed and quality of wool.

9. What is the Health Care of Sheep?

Under this component, there are provisions to take health care of sheep for the whole year to decrease the mortality rate and to make sheep healthier by providing medicine, vaccination treatment, dusting etc. so that the production of wool increases.

10. Who inspects the progress made under the SWIS?

A team constituted by the Board is doing the inspection.
