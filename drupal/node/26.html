Welcome to Central Wool Development Board. With a view to harmonize various diversified interests among different sectors of the wool industry for integrated policy development, the Central Wool Development Board was constituted in July 1987 with its headquarters at Jodhpur, Rajasthan. The Board has been functioning since 1989, and it is tasked with the growth and development of wool and woolens, marketing intelligence, price stabilization, testing, product development, and advising the government on policy matters.

Wool in India

In India, sheep rearing, spinning, and weaving of wool were part of Arya Life. The weaving and bleaching of wool are attributed to God Pasham, who was the god of shepherds. Wool is regarded as ceremonially pure for performing religious functions, used as a rug for prayers, and traditional wool 'Asan' is still used for Puja.

The ancient civilization of the Indus Valley revealed that our ancestors were skilled in wool weaving, spinning, felting, and tailoring. During the Muslim period, India became famous for fine Kashmir Woolen fabrics.

In early British days, attention was given to Pashmina shawls from Kashmir and Punjab, and efforts were made to improve indigenous sheep by crossing them with imported breeds. After independence, development programs were formulated under successive Five-Year Plans for sheep and wool development.

India has been famous for shawls and carpets, exported in large numbers to European countries during the medieval period. These were highly prized for their fine texture and excellent workmanship. Apart from luxury items, various other woolen goods such as blankets, lohis, patties, and tweeds were also made from indigenous wool for everyday use.

The Aims and Objectives

  • To promote sheep and wool development in the country.
  • To develop a wool marketing organization to stabilize wool prices.
  • To promote organized marketing through cooperative societies.
  • To provide training for wool quality improvement and marketing.
  • To support the woolen industry and ensure quality control.
  • To strengthen the sheep rearing industry.
  • To provide technological assistance for wool development.
