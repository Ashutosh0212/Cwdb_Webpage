CITIZENS' CHARTER OF CENTRAL WOOL DEVELOPMENT BOARD

This Charter is a declaration of our vision, mission, values, standards, and our commitment as an organization to promote the growth and development of wool and woollen products in the country.
We are an Autonomous Body under the Ministry of Textiles, Government of India, registered as a Society under the Societies Registration Act, managed by a Governing Body of 29 members representing the Central Government, State Governments, wool growers, and woollen textile industry.

VISION :

  • To improve the quality of Indian carpet-grade wool so that it matches the best in the world.
  • To provide better returns and more remunerative prices to wool growers and thereby improve their quality of life.

OUR MISSION :

To promote the growth and development of wool and woollen products.

OUR MANDATE :

In partnership with State Governmentâ€™s Wool Boards/Federations/Corporations/ Societies/NGOs and State Bodies
  • Implement programmes launched for the quality and growth of wool and woollen products with especial emphasis on the decentralized wool sector.
  • Facilitate the development of raw wool production, processing, and marketing of woollen products and provide testing and service facilities for the woollen sector.
  • Collect statistics and analyze and disseminate information on the Woollen sector to wool growers and wool producers.

OUR COMMITMENT :

We are committed to
  • Improving quality and quantity of indigenous raw wool and its optimum utilization.
  • Promoting or undertaking measures for better marketing and remunerative returns to wool growers.
  • Providing facilities for testing of wool and woollens
  • Propagating information useful to the growers, dealers of the wool, and manufacturers of Woollen products.
  • Facilitating and encouraging scientific, technological, and economic research into the matter of sheep husbandry practices, production, and processing of wool and woolens.
  • Improving and developing markets for woolen products

WE REACH OUT :

To wool growers, processors, weavers, artisans, and entrepreneurs, who are engaged in the production, processing, weaving, crafting and designing, testing, and services to the unorganized wool sector in the urban and rural areas of the country through
  • Animal/Sheep Husbandry Department of wool producing States.
  • State Government Organizations/NGOs engaged in wool development & marketing.
  • Wool Testing Centers.
  • Common Facility Centres for pre & post loom activities under Quality Processing of Wool
  • Non-Government Organizations
  • Weaving & Designing Training Centre.

     

OUR PROGRAMMES :

I. Integrated Wool Improvement and Development Programme (IWIDP) :
     A.  Sheep and Wool Improvement Scheme (SWIS)
     B.  Angora Wool Development Scheme
     C.  Pashmina Wool Development Scheme
     D.  Human Resource Development & Promotional activities

 

II. Quality Processing of Wool and Woollen Products :
     A.  Setting up of Common Facility Centre for wool scouring, carbonizing, carding, dying, Spinning, etc.
          (Pre-loom processing activity)
     B.  Setting up of Woollen Shawl/Carpet Finishing Centre (Post-loom processing activity)

 

III. Social Security Scheme for Sheep Breeders :
     A.  Sheep Breeders Insurance Scheme (Kendriya Bhed Palak Bima Yojana)
     B.  Sheep Insurance Scheme  (Kendriya Bhed Bima Yojana)
  • Projects for wool development in all wool producing States.
  • Projects for integrated Angora Wool Development Programme in hilly areas.
  • Projects for the development of Pashmina Wool in the Ladakh region.
  • Training programmes at all stages of sheep rearing, production, and processing of wool & woolens for improvement in quality & productivity.
  • Establishing CFCs for pre-loom & post loom activities under Quality Processing of Wool
  • Social Security Scheme to provide insurance coverage to sheep and sheep breeders.
  • Laboratories for Wool and Woollen Testing & Services.
  • Woollen Expos to provide a market-to-market exposure to unorganized woolen manufacturers with the small, tiny & household units.
