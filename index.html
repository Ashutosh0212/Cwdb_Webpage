Schemes

 

Wool Marketing Scheme

Sheep breeders belong to an unorganized sector who sell their wool in small volumes. 

In the present setup, sheep breeders are compelled to sell their produce at the prices dictated by traders due to their economic compulsions, low contribution of wool in the sheep economy & low volume, and distant location of markets. The prevailing system of wool marketing is less beneficial for the sheep breeders, and the main share of the profit goes to the middlemen. 

Therefore, there is a need to procure wool directly from the wool growers on a fixed price by the State Govt. organizations. Financial assistance would be provided to State Govt. organizations for procurement of wool at a fixed price directly from wool growers.

 

Human Resource Development & Promotional Activities 

 

Indian handloom-made woolen products are in high demand in domestic and international markets.

To manufacture these traditionally designed handloom products, special skills are required. Presently, in the country, there is a shortage of skilled workers to produce export-quality woolen handloom items. Under this component, it is intended to impart training in skill up-gradation and development in handloom/carpet weaving, knitting, and other woolen products manufacturing through training programmes. In the project, assistance for product development and design development with the designing software will also be provided.

Pashmina Wool Development Scheme

 

The aim of the sub-component is to purchase raw pashmina wool directly from pashmina growers at a fixed price to prevent exploitation by middlemen. 

The implementation of the component will also ensure the availability of raw pashmina wool to pashmina product manufacturers throughout the year at a cheaper rate.

Wool Processing Scheme

 

The Woollen industry suffers from inadequate and outdated processing facilities. The pre-loom and post-loom facilities are required to be modernized to ensure quality finished products. 

Quality finishing of the woolen products will not only increase the use of indigenous wool but will also make the product more competitive in the international market. It will also assist in procuring better prices for wool growers and will make quality raw materials available to the Khadi and Handloom sector. Owing to the overall size of the woolen industry and the specialized nature of equipments required for processing, the industry has been dependent on imported plants and machinery. 

With this view, setting up a Common Facility Centre for all kinds of wool and woolen processing facilities like wool scouring, drying, carding, dyeing, knitting, weaving, felting/non-woven, and finishing are proposed to be taken up by the CWDB for the integrated processing of raw wool and woolens in wool producing and wool trading areas. Establishing such plants/centers will increase wool processing capacity and will result in value addition to the Indian wool industry, and will create more employment opportunities and income generation in the country. Provision for the construction of buildings for housing the plant and machinery has also been made under this component.
